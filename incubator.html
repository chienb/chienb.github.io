CSUDH Incubator Migration & Redesign

CSUDH

2018

Provide an intuitive user experience by asking one simple but crucial question - what are you trying to achieve?

The Challenge/Problem

The Incubator was one of the micro sites that I created as part of the plan to migrate out of the current CMS as its own entity. The navigation needed some love as users were getting lost on the site and the programs were in locations where the hierarchy didn't make any sense.

One of the first things I noticed at the first glance was that the landing page was empty - it only had a newsletter signup CTA. Empty landing page = additional clicks to get info = bad UX. The right sidenav had no real hierarchy to guide users. It was confusing to see the Hatchery Program listed as a sub menu item under How to Apply while the other 2 programs are on the landing page.

Overall bounce rates were high, but the application page did well and users are spending almost 2 minutes on the about page. I presented my findings and scheduled a session with the director and his staff to brainstorm ideas for the new website.

Approach

The first question I always ask before starting any project is - what are you trying to achieve with the website? I believe this is the core element that will dictate how your website will look and function. A lot of times, I see designers and developers skip this crucial step in order to crank out websites on a tight deadline, only to come back to redo the whole thing. Sure, I get that sometimes people don't know what they want so there are no set goals. But my experiences have taught me that spending enough time to identify goals will help progress a project smoothly, so take as much time as you need.

Our goals were to:

  • Educate the public about the Incubator
  • Provide and clarify information on the programs
  • Generate interest to increase number of applications

I created a simple flow chart to illustrate the application process.

Design

I used the University's standard typeface and colors to maintain a cohesive brand image.

I mocked up the layout with sections to address the goals discussed earlier.

The homepage has a hero slider with CTA buttons that will encourage users to perform an action (signing up) with just one click. I also added a video presentation about the Incubator with a brief intro text.

The programs are now clearly laid out with an overall description and the available programs with button links for details. The clients section was also moved to the frontpage as a feature since some clients joined the program solely by looking at past clients and mentors. I also added and worded the link to staffs' page to encourage potential entrepreneurs that the program has helped a number of individuals achieve their dreams.

To improve the onboarding process, I created a landing page for Apply clearly labeling sections for clients and mentors respectively. The next step will bring them to the program page with an embedded Google signup form. Users will be able to complete an application and obtain information on page without being redirected.
