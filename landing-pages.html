Ad Campaign Landing Pages

CSUDH

2017

A mobile-first approach to designing landing pages that drive maximum enrollment

The Challenge/Problem

A lot has changed over the last thirty years in regards to college admissions. The Internet changed everything, and the education industry is no different. It changed the way students researched colleges and the way colleges advertised to students. Admission officers used to have more contact with students but now everything can be done online - viewing colleges, submitting applications, or getting financial aid without students ever setting a foot on campus. Educational institutions understand this and many create landing pages to convert prospects into leads.

CSUDH was one of the institutions that needed to replace its aging text-only landing pages and I was brought on to come up with a new design that would attract and ultimately convert leads into enrollment while adhering to ADA/WebAIM compliance rules.

Approach

After sitting down with the Director of Admissions and his staff, we were able to identify and set goals for the redesign:

  • Mobile-first - 90% of the traffic are on mobile devices from previous years
  • Increase brand awareness - use of official university branding elements, part of the campus-wide rebranding campaign
  • Tell a personal story - what are students doing with their education from CSUDH in short concise phrases
  • Simple landing page with a single submit form - remove links and unnecessary content to put focus on form submission
  • Follow ADA and WebAIM guidelines

Design

I created 3 wireframes, 2 had more page elements while the last design was very minimalistic. CSUDH preferred the latter approach

Areas of improvement

As we begun to roll out these new designs, I jotted down some notes for areas we could improve on for the next revision.

  • Removing logo hyperlink so the landing page doesn't provide visitors an exit link to leave the page, great if goal priority is conversions
  • More content could have been presented ie. program information, university stats callout, FAQ accordion, testimonials, or video clips
